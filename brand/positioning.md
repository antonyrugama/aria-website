# Aria — Brand Positioning & Context

> This file is the single source of truth for Aria's positioning. Reference it in all marketing, design, and product decisions.

## Positioning Statement

Aria is the AI-powered training companion built for athletes who train alone — combining sprint form analysis, adaptive programming, recovery guidance, and mental readiness into one intelligent system that develops with you, session after session.

---

## Core Message & Angle

**Angle:** The Solo Athlete's Intelligent Training Partner

Aria is not another analysis tool, calculator, or generic fitness app. It is a **companion** — an ever-present training partner that understands the reality of showing up to the track alone, making decisions without a coach, and pushing through barriers with no one in your corner.

**What makes this angle powerful:**
- Every competitor positions as a *tool* (upload video, get report, follow plan). Aria positions as a *relationship*.
- The word "companion" is unclaimed in the sprint-tech space.
- It reframes the value from "use this tool" to "train with Aria."
- It aligns perfectly with the name's meaning — a solo performance, grace under pressure, air and lightness in motion.

**Core narrative:**
> You train alone. You don't improve alone. Aria is the training partner that shows up every session — watching your form, building your plan, tracking your recovery, and keeping your mind sharp. Not a dashboard. Not a report. A companion that learns your body, adapts to your progress, and develops you the way a great coach would — session by session, week by week.

## Target Audience Profile

### Primary: The Self-Coached Competitive Sprinter (Ages 16–30)
- High school, college, or club-level track athletes competing at regional/national levels
- Training without a dedicated sprint coach, or with a coach stretched across 30–40 athletes
- Serious about improvement but structurally alone in their daily training
- Understands sprint mechanics conceptually but needs guided, progressive development
- Located anywhere — including countries where coaching infrastructure is thin

### Secondary: Masters Athletes (Ages 35+)
- Experienced competitors returning to the sport or maintaining performance
- Prioritize injury prevention, mobility, and training efficiency
- Value an intelligent system that respects their body's changing needs

### Tertiary: Performance-Focused Professionals (Ages 25–45)
- Fitness-conscious individuals maintaining structured routines
- Drawn to AI-backed coaching for speed, strength, and conditioning
- Appreciate premium, clean UX and data-driven guidance

### Emerging: Rehabilitation & Return-to-Training Users
- Athletes recovering from injuries or easing back into training
- Need gradual progression, mobility routines, and low-impact sprint alternatives

## Top 3 Pain Points We Address

### 1. "I train alone and have no one to coach me through it."
Solo athletes lack real-time feedback, session-to-session programming, and the accountability that comes with a coach. They're left guessing what to do, when to push, and when to pull back. Aria fills that void — not as a replacement coach, but as an intelligent partner that's always present.

### 2. "Tools tell me what's wrong, but not how to fix it."
Existing apps diagnose problems (poor hip flexion, over-striding) but leave athletes stranded after the report. There's no progressive path from diagnosis to correction to mastery. Aria doesn't just analyze — it builds a structured, evolving development curriculum: Detect > Educate > Drill > Re-assess > Progress.

### 3. "Nobody addresses the mental side of solo training."
Race-day anxiety, pre-competition doubt, motivation through plateaus, confidence after a bad session — zero competitors address these meaningfully. For solo athletes without a support system, the mental game is often the difference between a 10.3 in practice and a 10.3 in competition. Aria treats mental performance as a core feature, not a wellness afterthought.

## Tone of Voice Direction

### Personality Traits (from brand guidelines)

| Trait | In Practice |
|---|---|
| **Precise** | Data-informed guidance — never vague or generic. Every insight earns its place. |
| **Supportive** | A trusted training partner — focused, caring, reliable. Never preachy. |
| **Calm** | Professional and composed — never overwhelming, robotic, or hype-driven. |
| **Motivating** | Encourages progress and celebrates milestones with purpose, not empty enthusiasm. |
| **Human** | Warm, approachable — less technical jargon, more personal connection. |

### Voice principles:

1. **Speak like a training partner, not a product.** Say "Let's build on that" not "Your metrics have been uploaded."
2. **Be direct but never cold.** Confidence without arrogance. Guidance without condescension.
3. **Acknowledge the solo journey.** Aria understands what it means to train alone. Reflect that empathy in every touchpoint.
4. **Earn trust through specificity.** Vague encouragement ("You got this!") is cheap. Specific feedback ("Your ground contact improved 12ms this week — that's the drill work paying off") builds a real relationship.
5. **Never oversell.** No "revolutionary" or "game-changing." Let the product prove itself. Understate and overdeliver.

### Example voice lines:
- "That was a clean sprint — let's build on it tomorrow."
- "You've got everything you need. I'll keep you on track."
- "Your hip drive is 4 degrees off last week. Here's a drill sequence to correct it."
- "Rest day. Your body earned it. We'll come back sharper."

### What we NEVER sound like:
- Robotic or clinical ("Analysis complete. Error detected in biomechanical output.")
- Hype-driven or startup-bro ("Crush your goals! Level up! Beast mode!")
- Generic fitness app ("Great job! Keep going! You're amazing!")

## Product Capability Map (from TestFlight build, March 2026)

### Dual AI Intelligence System
Aria's core differentiator is a two-layer AI architecture — no competitor separates assessment from prescription:

**Aria IQ** — the sensing layer (what is my body doing today):
- Recovery status assessment ("Recovery Looks Strong")
- Body composition monitoring ("Monitor Body Mass Shift")
- Competition countdown awareness ("56 Days to Iberoamericano")
- Training load tracking (percentage + Low/Medium/High status)
- HRV, Resting Heart Rate, Sleep quality metrics

**AI Coach Insights** — the action layer (what should I do about it):
- Session prescriptions ("Schedule Max Speed Session")
- Nutrition adjustments ("Adjust Calories For Weight Loss")
- Periodization guidance ("Convert Strength to Power" with specific sets/reps)
- Program building ("Build 8-Week Peak Block")
- Competition-aware periodization (shifting from strength to race-specific speed based on goal date and current program week)

### AI Chat Coach
- Conversational interface with sprint-specific prompt suggestions
- Covers training analysis, programming, nutrition, recovery, and race strategy
- "Message Aria..." input with voice and attachment support

### Periodized Training Programs
- Structured multi-week blocks (e.g., "Periodo Preparatorio Especial 2" — 4 weeks, 6 workouts/week)
- Week-by-week navigation with active week indicator
- Daily sessions with specific exercises, sets, reps, rest periods, and intensity percentages
- Bilingual support (Spanish/English workout content)

### Training Log & Analytics
- Completed session tracking with timestamps, duration, exercise count
- RPE ratings with free-text athlete notes (AI reads these for context)
- Advanced Analytics: Training Load %, Heart Rate, Workout Frequency graphs
- Recent Workouts history with session details
- Progress & Analytics section

### Nutrition System
- Multiple phase-aware nutrition plans: Competition Day, In-Season, Off-Season Recovery
- Active/Archived plan management with one-tap switching
- Full daily meal plans: 6 meals timed from 5:30 AM to 8:00 PM
- Per-meal calorie and macro targets (P/C/F)
- Region-aware food suggestions (e.g., "Sprint Performance Fuel - Costa Rica")
- Nutrition Log with per-meal "Get AI Feedback" button
- AI-driven calorie/macro refinement tied to training phase and body composition goals

### Sprint Tools Suite
- Video Analysis
- Photo Finish
- Start Gun (audio cue for solo block practice)
- Stopwatch
- Sprint Predictor
- Timer
- Measure

### Additional Features
- Rehabilitation section
- Cycle Tracking (for female athletes — no competitor offers this)
- AI Coaching Style customization
- Voice Feedback setting
- Upcoming Events with competition goal times
- Sprint Times log
- Health Integrations: Apple Health, Strava, Garmin
- Dark mode UI with cyan/turquoise accent theming
